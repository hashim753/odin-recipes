Lasagna

Lasagna is a baked Italian dish built in layers: wide flat pasta sheets, rich sauce, creamy cheese, and a savory filling. The filling is commonly slow-cooked meat in tomato sauce or seasoned vegetables. Layers are stacked repeatedly, topped with cheese, and baked until the interior is hot and cohesive and the surface is browned. The result is dense, structured, and filling, with distinct layers that hold together when sliced.

Ingredients

    Lasagna ingredients (standard meat version)

  • Lasagna pasta sheets
  • Ground meat (beef or beef–pork mix)
  • Onion
  • Garlic
  • Tomato sauce or crushed tomatoes
  • Olive oil
  • Salt
  • Black pepper
  • Dried herbs (oregano, basil)

    • Cheese layer

  • Ricotta or béchamel sauce
  • Mozzarella cheese
  • Parmesan cheese

    • Optional

  • Egg (mixed into ricotta)
  • Red chili flakes
  • Fresh basil or parsley

Steps

  1. Boil lasagna sheets in salted water until just tender. Drain. Set flat.
  2. Heat olive oil. Sauté chopped onion until translucent. Add garlic.
  3. Add ground meat. Cook until browned.
  4. Add tomato sauce, salt, pepper, and herbs. Simmer until thick.
  5. Prepare cheese layer: mix ricotta (or keep béchamel ready).
  6. Preheat oven to ~180 °C.
  7. Assemble layers in a baking dish:
    • Sauce
    • Pasta sheets
    • Cheese
    • Repeat until used
  8. Finish with mozzarella and parmesan on top.
  9. Cover with foil. Bake ~25–30 minutes.
  10. Uncover. Bake 10–15 minutes until top is browned.
  11. Rest 10 minutes. Slice. Serve.
